JAMB Cut-Off Marks Guide (2026) for Nigerian Universities

Last Updated: April 2026

Many students ask one question first: what JAMB score is enough? The short answer is that your target should be higher than the official minimum, especially for competitive courses like Medicine, Law, Pharmacy, Nursing, and Engineering.

1. Understand the Three Layers

  • General JAMB benchmark set nationally
  • Institution-level baseline used for screening eligibility
  • Department-level competitiveness that shifts by year

2. Score Target Strategy

Use this safe framework instead of aiming for minimum only:

  • Competitive courses: target 250+ where possible
  • Mid-competitive courses: target 220 to 250
  • Less competitive courses: target 190 to 220 and focus on strong O'Level combination

3. Combine JAMB + WAEC + Post-UTME

Admission decisions are rarely from JAMB alone. Strong WAEC/NECO grades and good Post-UTME performance can significantly improve your standing.

4. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Choosing only one school and one highly crowded course without backup options
  • Ignoring subject combination rules for the intended program
  • Waiting too late to start Post-UTME preparation

5. Practical Action Plan

  1. Set a realistic score target based on your course competition level.
  2. Build a weekly revision timetable with past-question blocks.
  3. Keep a second-choice course and institution strategy ready.
  4. Use your first-year CGPA plan early if admission is secured, so you start strong from 100 level.
