The Ultimate Guide to CGPA Calculation in Nigerian Tertiary Institutions

Last Updated: March 2026

Understanding your Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) is crucial for academic success in Nigeria. Whether you are a student at the University of Ibadan, UNILAG, OAU, UNIPORT e.t.c or leading Polytechnics, your CGPA is the primary metric used to evaluate your academic performance throughout your stay in school. This guide provides a comprehensive breakdown of how these systems work and how you can manage yours effectively.

1. What is GPA and CGPA?

Before diving into the scales, it is important to distinguish between GPA and CGPA. The Grade Point Average (GPA) is the result of your performance in a single semester. In contrast, the Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) is the mean of all your GPAs from the very first semester until your current level.

In Nigeria, the calculation is weighted based on "Credit Units." A 4-unit course carries more weight than a 1-unit course. Therefore, getting an 'A' in a 4-unit course will boost your CGPA much more than getting an 'A' in a 1-unit elective.

2. Common Grading Scales in Nigeria

Nigeria uses three major grading scales depending on the institution and the year of entry.

The 5.0 CGPA Scale (Most Universities)

This is the standard for most federal and state universities in Nigeria. Under this system, grades are typically assigned the following points:

  • A (70-100%): 5 Points
  • B (60-69%): 4 Points
  • C (50-59%): 3 Points
  • D (45-49%): 2 Points
  • E (40-44%): 1 Point
  • F (0-39%): 0 Points

The 4.0 CGPA Scale (Polytechnics & New NUC Standard)

The National Board for Technical Education (NBTE) uses the 4.0 scale for Polytechnics (ND and HND programs). Additionally, the National Universities Commission (NUC) recently recommended a transition to the 4.0 scale for universities to align with global standards, though many still use the 5.0 scale.

  • A: 4.0 Points
  • AB/B+: 3.5 Points
  • B: 3.0 Points
  • BC/C+: 2.5 Points
  • C: 2.0 Points
  • CD/D+: 1.5 Points
  • D: 1.0 Points
  • F: 0.0 Points

The 7.0 CGPA Scale (Legacy University Systems)

The 7.0 scale is rarely in use in Nigeria. It was primarily used by the University of Ibadan (UI) and its affiliated institutions.

3. How to Calculate Your CGPA Manually

To calculate your GPA for a semester, follow these steps:

  1. Multiply the Credit Units of each course by the Grade Points earned (e.g., a 3-unit course with an 'A' on a 5.0 scale is 3 x 5 = 15 Total Points).
  2. Sum up all the Total Points for the semester. This is your Total Quality Points (TQP).
  3. Sum up all the Credit Units for the semester. This is your Total Credit Units (TCU).
  4. Divide TQP by TCU (GPA = TQP / TCU).

For the CGPA, you simply sum the TQP of all semesters and divide by the sum of the TCU of all semesters.

4. Degree Classifications in Nigeria

On a standard 5.0 scale, the classifications are usually:

  • 4.50 – 5.00: First Class Honours
  • 3.50 – 4.49: Second Class Honours (Upper Division)
  • 2.40 – 3.49: Second Class Honours (Lower Division)
  • 1.50 – 2.39: Third Class Honours
  • 1.00 – 1.49: Pass
  • 0.00 – 0.99: Fail

5. Strategies for Maintaining a High CGPA

Maintaining a high CGPA requires consistency and strategy. Here are three expert tips for Nigerian students:

  • Focus on High-Unit Courses: As mentioned earlier, 3-unit and 4-unit courses define your GPA. Ensure you secure at least a 'B' in these core courses.
  • The "100 Level" Rule: It is much easier to maintain a high CGPA than to build one up from a low starting point. Aim for your best results in 100 level and 200 level.
